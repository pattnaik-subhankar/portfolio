Architecture Principles

Five principles forged in production firefights. These govern every design decision I make.

  1. Design for Failure First

    Every component assumes its dependencies will fail. Circuit breakers, retry policies with exponential backoff, and graceful degradation are not afterthoughts — they are the starting point.

  2. Infrastructure Is Code, Always

    No click-ops. Every resource, every policy, every network rule is defined in Terraform or Bicep and version-controlled. Drift detection and reconciliation are automated.

  3. Least Privilege by Default

    Managed identities over connection strings. Private endpoints over public access. RBAC roles scoped to minimum required permissions. Zero standing access to production.

  4. Observability Is Not Optional

    Every component emits structured logs, metrics, and distributed traces. Dashboards are built before the first deployment. If you can't see it, you can't fix it.

  5. Cost Is an Architectural Concern

    Budget enforcement via Azure Policy. Auto-scaling that scales down as aggressively as it scales up. Reserved instances where steady-state exists. Every dollar spent must earn its place.
