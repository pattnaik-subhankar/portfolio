My Approach

Architecture Philosophy

Principles that guide every decision — forged in production incidents and validated across industries.

🏛️ The Principles

Design for failure, not perfection. Every service degrades. The question is whether your architecture degrades gracefully or catastrophically. I design for the latter by default — circuit breakers, retry storms, queue backpressure — because I've seen what happens when you don't.
Private by default. If it doesn't need internet access, it shouldn't have it. Private endpoints, VNet integration, internal-mode APIM — not as an afterthought, but as the starting point of every architecture diagram.
Infrastructure is code, or it's a liability. Every resource in every environment must be repeatable. Bicep for Azure-native, Terraform for multi-cloud — both with policy-as-code guardrails that prevent drift before it happens.
Observability isn't optional. If you can't see it, you can't fix it. Distributed tracing, structured logging, alerting on SLOs rather than symptoms — built in from day one, not bolted on after the first outage.
Cost is an architecture concern. A solution that costs $50K/month but could cost $15K/month is a design failure. Every architecture decision includes a cost model — because nobody thanks you for a beautiful design that bankrupts the project.
